{% trans "Huntington's disease is a rare, genetic disease that leads to the gradual destruction of brain cells. This is an incurable disease that usually manifests itself in middle age, but can begin earlier." %}

{% trans "Symptoms" %}

{% trans "The symptoms of Huntington's disease are diverse and can progress over time. The main groups of symptoms include:" %}

  • {% trans "Motor disorders: involuntary movements, coordination problems, slow movements, muscle stiffness." %}
  • {% trans "Cognitive impairment: problems with memory, attention, decision-making, difficulties with planning." %}
  • {% trans "Mental disorders: depression, irritability, aggressiveness, personality changes." %}

{% trans "Description" %}

{% trans "The disease is caused by a gene mutation that leads to the accumulation of abnormal protein in brain cells. This protein damages cells, which leads to their death." %}
